Differential Privacy: Introduction

Differential Privacy ensures individual privacy by adding random noise to queries. Adjust epsilon and sensitivity values below to see how they affect the amount of noise added.

Instructions:

  1. Adjust the epsilon and sensitivity values using the sliders below. These values control the amount of noise added to protect privacy.

  2. Click "Apply To Numerical" to apply differential privacy to numerical data and observe how noise affects numeric results.

  3. Click "Apply To Categorical" to apply differential privacy to categorical data (e.g., Marital Status) and display the noisy distribution.

  4. Compare the original and noisy distributions displayed below to understand how differential privacy alters data.

Sensitivity is set to 1 for counting queries, but you can adjust it here for demonstration purposes.
